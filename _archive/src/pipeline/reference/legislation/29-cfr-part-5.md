# 29 CFR Part 5 - Labor Standards Provisions

**Title**: Labor Standards Provisions Applicable to Contracts Covering Federally Financed Construction

**Agency**: Department of Labor
**Citation**: 29 CFR Part 5
**Last Updated**: Various amendments

## Key Subparts

### Subpart A - General Provisions

**§ 5.1 Purpose and Scope**
This part sets forth the labor standards provisions applicable to contracts covering federally financed construction.

**§ 5.2 Definitions**
- **Contract**: Any agreement between a contractor and a contracting agency
- **Construction**: Includes construction, alteration, or repair of public buildings or public works
- **Laborer or Mechanic**: Workers employed in construction

### Subpart B - Contract Clauses

**§ 5.5 Contract Clauses**

**§ 5.5(a) Davis-Bacon Act**
Every contract in excess of $2,000 must include Davis-Bacon provisions.

**§ 5.5(a)(1) Wage Determinations**
Contract must specify minimum wages for each classification of laborer and mechanic.

**§ 5.5(a)(2) Weekly Payroll Submission**
Contractor must submit weekly payrolls to the contracting agency.

**§ 5.5(a)(3) Payroll Requirements**
Payrolls must include:
- Name, address, and Social Security number of each employee
- Correct classification of work performed
- Hours worked each day
- Hourly rate of pay
- Daily and weekly gross pay
- Deductions made
- Net wages paid

**§ 5.5(a)(4) Classification**
Workers must be classified according to the work actually performed.

### Subpart C - Wage Determinations

**§ 5.22 Determination of Prevailing Wage Rates**

**§ 5.22(a) General**
The Secretary of Labor determines prevailing wage rates based on surveys of wages paid in the area.

**§ 5.22(b) Components of Wage Rate**
Prevailing wage rate includes:
- Basic hourly rate
- Fringe benefits (or cash equivalent)
- Total rate = basic rate + fringe rate

**§ 5.22(c) Wage Determination Types**
- General determination: Applies to all covered contracts in an area
- Project determination: Specific to a particular project
- Area determination: Specific to a geographic area

### Subpart D - Compliance

**§ 5.5(a)(5) Compliance Monitoring**
Contracting agencies monitor compliance through:
- Payroll reviews
- Site inspections
- Worker interviews
- Record audits

**§ 5.5(a)(6) Violations**
Contractors found in violation may face:
- Withholding of payments
- Contract termination
- Debarment from future contracts
- Civil penalties

## Key Requirements for WCP Compliance

### 1. Payroll Submission
- Weekly submission required
- Must include all required fields
- Must be certified as accurate
- Must be submitted to contracting agency

### 2. Classification
- Must match actual work performed
- Cannot use lower-paid classification
- Must follow DBWD wage determination classifications

### 3. Wage Payment
- Must pay at least prevailing wage rate
- Must include fringe benefits or cash equivalent
- Must pay overtime for hours over 40

### 4. Record Keeping
- Must maintain payroll records for 3 years
- Records must be available for inspection
- Must support wage and classification decisions
