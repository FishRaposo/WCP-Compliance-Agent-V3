# 40 U.S.C. § 3145 - Copeland "Anti-Kickback" Act

**Full Title**: An Act to prevent persons from inducing laborers and mechanics to give up any part of the compensation to which they are entitled

**Enacted**: 1934
**Last Amended**: Various amendments
**Citation**: 40 U.S.C. § 3145

## Full Text (Excerpt - Relevant Sections)

### Weekly Payroll Certification

(a) The contractor shall submit weekly a copy of all payrolls to the Federal agency contracting for or financing the construction project. The payrolls shall set out accurately and completely the following information for each laborer or mechanic:
- (1) Name, address, and Social Security number
- (2) Correct classification of the work performed
- (3) Hours worked each day
- (4) Hourly rate of pay
- (5) Daily and weekly gross pay
- (6) Deductions made
- (7) Net wages paid

### Certification Requirement

(b) The payroll shall be accompanied by a statement signed by the contractor or the contractor's authorized representative, certifying that:
- (1) The payroll is correct and complete
- (2) The wage rates contained therein are not less than those required by the contract
- (3) The classifications set forth for each laborer or mechanic are consistent with the work performed
- (4) The contractor has paid each laborer or mechanic not less than the required wages

### Anti-Kickback Provision

(c) No contractor or subcontractor shall induce any laborer or mechanic to give up any part of the compensation to which they are entitled under the contract.

### Record Retention

(d) All payroll records shall be preserved for a period of three years from the completion of the contract.

## Key Requirements for WCP Compliance

### 1. Weekly Submission
- Payrolls must be submitted weekly
- Must be signed/certified by authorized representative
- Must include all required information

### 2. Certification Accuracy
- Certification must be truthful
- False certification is a violation
- Signature indicates legal responsibility

### 3. No Kickbacks
- Contractors cannot force workers to give up wages
- No illegal deductions
- Full wages must be paid

### 4. Record Retention
- Records kept for 3 years after contract completion
- Must be available for inspection
- Includes payrolls, certifications, and supporting documents
