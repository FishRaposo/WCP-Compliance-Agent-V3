# 40 U.S.C. § 3702 - Contract Work Hours and Safety Standards Act (CWHSSA)

**Full Title**: An Act to provide for the safety and health of workers engaged in construction work and to provide for the payment of overtime compensation

**Enacted**: 1962
**Last Amended**: Various amendments
**Citation**: 40 U.S.C. § 3702

## Full Text (Excerpt - Relevant Sections)

### Overtime Requirements

(a) No contractor or subcontractor contracting for any part of the contract work which may require or involve the employment of laborers or mechanics shall require or permit any such laborer or mechanic to work in excess of 40 hours in any one week unless such laborer or mechanic receives compensation for all hours worked in excess of 40 hours at a rate not less than one and one-half times the basic rate of pay for such work.

### Calculation of Overtime

(b) For the purpose of this section, the basic rate of pay shall be the rate of pay the laborer or mechanic would have received had the overtime work not been performed.

### Exemptions

(c) The provisions of this section shall not apply with respect to:
- (1) Any laborer or mechanic employed by a contractor or subcontractor performing work on a contract which is exempt from the Davis-Bacon Act
- (2) Any laborer or mechanic employed in an executive, administrative, or professional capacity

## Key Requirements for WCP Compliance

### 1. Overtime Threshold
- Standard workweek: 40 hours
- Hours over 40 must be paid at 1.5x rate
- Applies to all laborers and mechanics on covered contracts

### 2. Overtime Rate
- Overtime rate = 1.5 × base hourly rate
- Fringe benefits not included in overtime calculation
- Must be paid for all hours over 40 in a workweek

### 3. Record Keeping
- Overtime hours must be tracked separately
- Overtime pay must be clearly identified
- Records must support overtime calculations

### 4. No Waiver
- Workers cannot waive overtime rights
- No "comp time" instead of overtime pay
- Overtime pay is mandatory
